LIGHT: REFLECTION & REFRACTION

PAGE 1
Nature of Light
Light: It is a form of energy that enables us to see objects. It travels in a straight line (Rectilinear propagation).
Dual Nature: Light exhibits both wave nature and particle nature (Quantum theory).
PAGE 2
Reflection of Light
Reflection: Bouncing back of light when it strikes a polished surface.
Laws of Reflection:
1. Angle of incidence ($i$) = Angle of reflection ($r$).
2. Incident ray, reflected ray, and normal all lie in the same plane.
PAGE 3
Spherical Mirrors
Concave Mirror: Reflecting surface is curved inwards. It is a converging mirror.
Convex Mirror: Reflecting surface is curved outwards. It is a diverging mirror.
PAGE 4
Important Terms
Principal Axis: Line passing through Pole (P) and Centre of Curvature (C).
Focal Length ($f$): Distance between Pole and Focus. Relationship: $R = 2f$.
PAGE 5
Image Formation (Concave)
Concave mirrors form both Real & Inverted and Virtual & Erect images depending on object position.
Key Position: When object is between P and F, the image is virtual, erect, and magnified (Used by dentists).
PAGE 6
Mirror Formula & Magnification
Formula: $\frac{1}{v} + \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{f}$
Magnification ($m$): $m = \frac{h'}{h} = -\frac{v}{u}$.
If $m$ is negative, image is real. If $m$ is positive, image is virtual.
PAGE 7
Refraction of Light
Refraction: Bending of light when it travels from one medium to another due to change in speed.
Snell's Law: $\frac{\sin i}{\sin r} = \text{constant}$ (Refractive Index).
PAGE 8
Refractive Index ($n$)
Relative Index: $n_{21} = \frac{\text{Speed of light in medium 1}}{\text{Speed of light in medium 2}}$
Absolute Index: $n_m = \frac{c}{v}$ (where $c = 3 \times 10^8$ m/s).
PAGE 9
Spherical Lenses
Convex Lens: Converging lens. Thicker at the middle.
Concave Lens: Diverging lens. Thicker at the edges.
PAGE 10
Lens Formula & Power
Lens Formula: $\frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{f}$
Power ($P$): $P = \frac{1}{f \text{ (in meters)}}$. SI Unit: Dioptre (D). Convex is (+), Concave is (-).

-- LONG QUESTIONS --

LONG Q1
Define the following terms in relation to spherical mirrors: (a) Pole, (b) Centre of Curvature, (c) Principal Axis, (d) Principal Focus.

Answer:
1. Pole (P): The centre of the reflecting surface of a spherical mirror.
2. Centre of Curvature (C): The centre of the hollow sphere of which the mirror forms a part.
3. Principal Axis: A straight line passing through the pole and the centre of curvature of a spherical mirror.
4. Principal Focus (F): A point on the principal axis where all rays parallel to the principal axis actually meet (concave) or appear to meet (convex) after reflection.

LONG Q2
An object 4 cm in size is placed at 25 cm in front of a concave mirror of focal length 15 cm. At what distance from the mirror should a screen be placed to obtain a sharp image? Find the nature and size of the image.

Answer: Given: $h = 4$ cm, $u = -25$ cm, $f = -15$ cm (Concave mirror).
Using Mirror Formula: $\frac{1}{v} + \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{f}$
$\frac{1}{v} = \frac{1}{-15} - \frac{1}{-25} = \frac{-5 + 3}{75} = \frac{-2}{75}$
$v = -37.5$ cm. (Screen should be placed at 37.5 cm in front of mirror).

Magnification: $m = -\frac{v}{u} = -\frac{-37.5}{-25} = -1.5$
Since $m$ is negative, image is Real and Inverted.
Size: $h' = m \times h = -1.5 \times 4 = -6$ cm (Enlarged).

LONG Q3
What is refraction of light? State the laws of refraction. What is the meaning of absolute refractive index?

Answer: Refraction is the bending of light when it enters obliquely from one transparent medium to another.
Laws of Refraction:
1. The incident ray, the refracted ray and the normal all lie in the same plane.
2. Snell’s Law: The ratio of sine of angle of incidence to the sine of angle of refraction is constant ($\frac{\sin i}{\sin r} = n$).

Absolute Refractive Index: It is the ratio of the speed of light in vacuum ($c$) to the speed of light in a specific medium ($v$). Formula: $n = \frac{c}{v}$.

LONG Q4
Draw ray diagrams to show the image formation by a concave mirror when the object is placed: (a) Between P and F, (b) At C. State the characteristics of the image in each case.

Answer:
(a) Object between P and F:
- Position: Behind the mirror.
- Nature: Virtual and Erect.
- Size: Magnified.

(b) Object at C:
- Position: At C.
- Nature: Real and Inverted.
- Size: Same size as the object.

LONG Q5
Define power of a lens. What is its SI unit? One student uses a lens of focal length +50 cm and another of -50 cm. State the nature and power of each lens.

Answer: Power of a lens is the degree of convergence or divergence of light rays achieved by it. It is the reciprocal of focal length in metres ($P = \frac{1}{f}$).
- SI Unit: Dioptre (D).

Case 1: $f = +50$ cm $= +0.5$ m
- Power: $P = \frac{1}{0.5} = +2$ D.
- Nature: Convex Lens (Converging).

Case 2: $f = -50$ cm $= -0.5$ m
- Power: $P = \frac{1}{-0.5} = -2$ D.
- Nature: Concave Lens (Diverging).

LONG Q6
Explain the sign convention for reflection by spherical mirrors (New Cartesian Sign Convention).

Answer: According to the New Cartesian Sign Convention:
1. The object is always placed to the left of the mirror.
2. All distances parallel to the principal axis are measured from the Pole (P).
3. Distances measured in the direction of incident light (right of P) are positive (+).
4. Distances measured against the direction of incident light (left of P) are negative (-).
5. Heights measured upwards and perpendicular to the principal axis are positive (+).
6. Heights measured downwards are negative (-).

LONG Q7
State the lens formula and magnification. A convex lens of focal length 10 cm is placed at a distance of 15 cm from a wall. How far from the lens should an object be placed to form its real image on the wall?

Answer: Lens Formula: $\frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{f}$ | Magnification: $m = \frac{v}{u}$.

Numerical: Given $f = +10$ cm (Convex), $v = +15$ cm (Image on wall is real).
$\frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{15} - \frac{1}{10}$
$\frac{1}{u} = \frac{2 - 3}{30} = \frac{-1}{30}$
$u = -30$ cm.
The object should be placed 30 cm in front of the lens.

LONG Q8
Differentiate between a Convex lens and a Concave lens on the basis of: (a) Shape, (b) Action on light rays, (c) Nature of image formed.

Answer:
1. Convex Lens:
- (a) Thicker at middle, thinner at edges.
- (b) Converging action.
- (c) Forms both real and virtual images.

2. Concave Lens:
- (a) Thinner at middle, thicker at edges.
- (b) Diverging action.
- (c) Always forms a virtual, erect, and diminished image.

LONG Q9
Refractive index of water is 1.33 and that of glass is 1.50. (i) In which medium does light travel faster? (ii) What is the refractive index of glass with respect to water?

Answer:
(i) Light travels faster in Water because it has a lower refractive index (1.33) compared to glass (1.50). Speed of light is inversely proportional to refractive index.

(ii) Refractive index of glass w.r.t. water ($n_{gw}$):
$n_{gw} = \frac{n_g}{n_w} = \frac{1.50}{1.33} \approx 1.127$.

LONG Q10
State three uses each of: (a) Concave mirror, (b) Convex mirror.

Answer:
(a) Concave Mirror Uses:
1. Used in torches and search-lights to get powerful parallel beams.
2. Used as shaving mirrors to see a larger image of the face.
3. Used by dentists to see large images of teeth.

(b) Convex Mirror Uses:
1. Used as rear-view mirrors in vehicles because they give an erect image and a wider field of view.
2. Used as security mirrors in shops and ATMs.
3. Used in street lights to diverge light over a large area.

-- HARD QUESTIONS --

HARD Q11
A convex lens of focal length 20 cm can produce a magnified as well as a diminished real image. Is this a true statement? Justify your answer with ray diagrams (positions).

Answer: Yes, the statement is True. A convex lens forms real images of different sizes based on the object's position:
1. For Magnified Real Image: Place the object between F and 2F. The image formed will be beyond 2F, real, inverted, and larger than the object.
2. For Diminished Real Image: Place the object beyond 2F. The image will be formed between F and 2F on the other side, and it will be real, inverted, and smaller than the object.
3. Note: At 2F, the image is of the same size. Thus, a convex lens is very versatile.

HARD Q12
The refractive indices of three media A, B and C are 1.33, 1.65 and 1.46 respectively. In which medium is the speed of light (i) Maximum, (ii) Minimum? Also, find the refractive index of B with respect to A.

Answer: Speed of light ($v$) is inversely proportional to the refractive index ($n$). ($v = c/n$)

(i) Maximum Speed: Medium A ($n = 1.33$). Since it has the lowest refractive index, the speed of light will be highest here.
(ii) Minimum Speed: Medium B ($n = 1.65$). Since it has the highest refractive index, light will travel slowest here.

Relative Refractive Index ($n_{BA}$):
$n_{BA} = \frac{n_B}{n_A} = \frac{1.65}{1.33} \approx 1.24$.

HARD Q13
An object is placed at a distance of 10 cm from a convex mirror of focal length 15 cm. Find the position and nature of the image. Why is this mirror preferred as a rear-view mirror?

Answer: Given: $u = -10$ cm, $f = +15$ cm (Convex mirror).
$\frac{1}{v} + \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{f} \implies \frac{1}{v} = \frac{1}{15} - \frac{1}{-10} = \frac{1}{15} + \frac{1}{10}$
$\frac{1}{v} = \frac{2 + 3}{30} = \frac{5}{30} \implies v = +6$ cm.
Image is formed 6 cm behind the mirror.
Nature: Virtual and Erect (since $v$ is positive and $m < 1$).

Why Rear-View?
1. It always gives an erect image.
2. It has a wider field of view as it is curved outwards, allowing the driver to see much more traffic than a plane mirror.

HARD Q14
Two lenses of power +3.5 D and -2.5 D are placed in contact. Find the power and focal length of the combination. What is the nature of the resultant lens?

Answer: Total Power ($P$) = $P_1 + P_2$
$P = (+3.5) + (-2.5) = +1.0$ D.

Focal Length ($f$):
$f = \frac{1}{P} = \frac{1}{1.0} = 1$ metre or $100$ cm.

Nature: Since the total power is positive, the combination behaves as a Convex Lens (Converging lens).

HARD Q15
A concave mirror of focal length 20 cm forms an image having twice the size of the object. For the two possible positions of the object, find the distance between them.

Answer: Here $f = -20$ cm and $|m| = 2$.
Case 1 (Real Image): $m = -2 \implies -v/u = -2 \implies v = 2u$.
$1/2u + 1/u = 1/-20 \implies 3/2u = -1/20 \implies u_1 = -30$ cm.
Case 2 (Virtual Image): $m = +2 \implies -v/u = 2 \implies v = -2u$.
$1/-2u + 1/u = 1/-20 \implies 1/2u = -1/20 \implies u_2 = -10$ cm.
Distance between positions: $|u_1 - u_2| = | -30 - (-10) | = 20$ cm.

HARD Q16
Explain with a diagram why a pencil partially immersed in water appears bent at the interface. Which property of light is responsible for this?

Answer: This happens due to Refraction of Light.
When light rays coming from the immersed part of the pencil travel from water (denser) to air (rarer), they bend away from the normal. When these refracted rays reach our eyes, they appear to come from a point slightly higher than the actual position. This shift in the apparent position of the submerged part makes the pencil look bent at the surface.

HARD Q17
What is 'Lateral Displacement'? List the factors on which the lateral displacement of a ray of light passing through a rectangular glass slab depends.

Answer: The perpendicular distance between the original path of the incident ray and the emergent ray is called Lateral Displacement.
Factors:
1. Thickness of glass slab: More thickness = More displacement.
2. Refractive Index: Higher RI = More displacement.
3. Angle of Incidence: Larger angle = More displacement.
4. Wavelength of light: Violet light deviates more than red light.

HARD Q18
Define one Dioptre. A lens has a power of -5D. If an object is placed at 40 cm from the lens, what is the position and nature of the image?

Answer: 1 Dioptre is the power of a lens whose focal length is 1 metre.
Numerical: $P = -5D \implies f = 1/P = 1/-5 = -0.2$ m or $-20$ cm.
Given $u = -40$ cm. Using Lens Formula: $1/v - 1/u = 1/f$
$1/v = 1/-20 + 1/-40 = (-2-1)/40 = -3/40$.
$v = -13.33$ cm.
Nature: Virtual, Erect, and Diminished (Concave lens).
