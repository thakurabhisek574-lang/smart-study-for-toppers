CHAPTER 1: CHEMICAL REACTIONS & EQUATIONS

PAGE 1: BASICS

What is a Chemical Reaction?

It is a process where Reactants transform into Products with entirely new properties.

  • Breaking and making of chemical bonds.
  • Atoms are rearranged, not destroyed.
Reactants (LHS) → Products (RHS)
PAGE 2: IDENTIFICATION

Observations in a Reaction

How do we know a reaction happened?

  • Evolution of Gas: Zn + H2SO4 → ZnSO4 + H2↑
  • Change in State: Burning of wax (Solid to Gas).
  • Change in Temperature: Slaked lime formation (Exothermic).
  • Formation of Precipitate: BaCl2 + Na2SO4 → BaSO4↓
PAGE 3: ACTIVITY 1.1

Burning of Magnesium Ribbon

Magnesium burns with a Dazzling White Flame to form white Magnesium Oxide powder.

2Mg(s) + O2(g) → 2MgO(s)

Note: Sandpaper is used to remove the layer of basic magnesium carbonate before burning.

PAGE 4: MATHEMATICS

Balanced Chemical Equations

Must satisfy Law of Conservation of Mass. Total mass of reactants = Total mass of products.

  • Number of atoms of each element must be same on both sides.
  • Always use coefficients, never change the formula.
PAGE 5: TYPES - I

Combination Reaction

Single product is formed from two or more reactants.

CaO(s) + H2O(l) → Ca(OH)2(aq) + Heat

Quick lime reacts with water to give Slaked Lime. This is highly exothermic.

PAGE 6: TYPES - II

Decomposition Reaction

A single reactant breaks down into multiple products. Needs energy.

  • Thermal: Heat energy (Lead Nitrate brown fumes).
  • Electrolytic: Electric energy (Water electrolysis 2:1 ratio).
  • Photolytic: Light energy (Silver Chloride grey turns).
PAGE 7: TYPES - III

Displacement Reactions

More reactive element kicks out less reactive element from its salt solution.

Fe(s) + CuSO4(aq) → FeSO4(aq) + Cu(s)

Iron nail turns brownish and blue Copper Sulphate turns Light Green.

PAGE 8: TYPES - IV

Double Displacement

Exchange of ions between reactants. Usually forms a Precipitate.

Na2SO4 + BaCl2 → BaSO4(↓) + 2NaCl
PAGE 9: REDOX

Oxidation and Reduction

  • Oxidation: Add Oxygen / Remove Hydrogen.
  • Reduction: Remove Oxygen / Add Hydrogen.
CuO + H2 → Cu + H2O

CuO is reduced; H2 is oxidized.

PAGE 10: APPLICATIONS

Corrosion & Rancidity

Corrosion: Damage to metals by air/moisture (Rusting, Black silver, Green copper).

Rancidity: Oxidation of fats/oils in food. Change in smell/taste.

Prevention: Antioxidants, Nitrogen gas in chips, Airtight containers.

SECTION C: CHAPTER 1 LONG QUESTIONS

Q1. Explain the different types of Decomposition Reactions with one example each.
1. Thermal Decomposition: Carried out by heating.
Example: CaCO3 (s) → CaO (s) + CO2 (g)

2. Electrolytic Decomposition: Carried out by electricity.
Example: 2H2O (l) → 2H2 (g) + O2 (g)

3. Photolytic Decomposition: Carried out by sunlight.
Example: 2AgCl (s) → 2Ag (s) + Cl2 (g)
Q2. What is a Redox Reaction? Identify the substance oxidized and reduced in: CuO + H2 → Cu + H2O.
Definition: A reaction in which oxidation and reduction take place simultaneously is called a Redox reaction.

In the given reaction:
1. H2 is getting Oxidized to H2O (Addition of Oxygen).
2. CuO is getting Reduced to Cu (Removal of Oxygen).

Oxidizing Agent: CuO | Reducing Agent: H2
Q3. Why is Respiration considered an Exothermic reaction? Explain.
Reason: During digestion, food is broken down into glucose. This glucose combines with oxygen in the cells of our body and provides energy.

Equation: C6H12O6 + 6O2 → 6CO2 + 6H2O + Energy

Since energy is released in this process, respiration is called an Exothermic reaction.
Q4. Differentiate between Displacement and Double Displacement reactions with equations.
Displacement Reaction: A more reactive element displaces a less reactive element from its salt solution.
Equation: Fe + CuSO4 → FeSO4 + Cu

Double Displacement: There is an exchange of ions between the reactants to form new compounds.
Equation: Na2SO4 + BaCl2 → BaSO4 + 2NaCl
Q5. What is the difference between Exothermic and Endothermic reactions? Give examples.
Exothermic Reaction: Reactions in which heat is released along with the formation of products.
Example: Burning of natural gas: CH4 + 2O2 → CO2 + 2H2O + Heat.

Endothermic Reaction: Reactions which require energy in the form of heat, light, or electricity to break reactants.
Example: Decomposition of Calcium Carbonate: CaCO3 + Heat → CaO + CO2.
Q6. What is Corrosion? List three methods to prevent rusting of iron.
Corrosion: It is the process in which metals are eaten up gradually by the action of air, moisture, or chemicals on their surface.

Prevention Methods:
1. Painting: Applying a coat of paint cuts off air and moisture contact.
2. Galvanisation: Coating iron with a thin layer of Zinc.
3. Greasing/Oiling: Applying grease to machinery parts to prevent contact with moisture.
Q7. Explain Rancidity. How can it be prevented in food items?
Rancidity: The oxidation of fats and oils in food when kept for a long time, resulting in bad smell and taste.

Prevention:
1. Adding Antioxidants to foods containing fats.
2. Storing food in Air-tight containers to slow down oxidation.
3. Flushing food packets (like chips) with Nitrogen gas.
Q8. Why do we apply paint on iron articles? Why are oil and fat containing food items flushed with Nitrogen?
Iron Articles: Paint prevents contact with air and moisture, which stops rusting and increases the life of the article.

Nitrogen Flushing: Nitrogen is an inert gas. It prevents the oil and fats in food from getting oxidized, keeping the food fresh.
