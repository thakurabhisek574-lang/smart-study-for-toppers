CHAPTER 10: THE HUMAN EYE

PAGE 1: ANATOMY

1. Structure & Parts of the Eye

The human eye is the most sensitive sense organ. It uses a Convex Lens to form a real, inverted image on the Retina.

Main Parts:
- Cornea: Outer layer jahan maximum refraction hota hai.
- Iris: Pupil ka size control karta hai.
- Ciliary Muscles: Lens ki focal length adjust karti hain.
- Retina: Screen jahan image banti hai (Rods & Cones cells).
PAGE 2: FOCUSING

2. Power of Accommodation

Eye lens ki focal length ko badalne ki ability ko Accommodation kehte hain. Yeh Ciliary muscles ki wajah se hota hai.

Points to Remember:
- Near Point: 25 cm (Minimum distance for clear vision).
- Far Point: Infinity ($\infty$).
- Budhape mein yeh power kam ho jati hai (Presbyopia).
PAGE 3-5: DEFECTS

3. Myopia & Hypermetropia

Jab image retina par nahi banti, toh vision defects paida hote hain. Inhe lenses se theek kiya jata hai.

1. Myopia (Short-sightedness): Door ka nahi dikhta. Image retina ke aage banti hai. Correction: Concave Lens.

2. Hypermetropia (Far-sightedness): Paas ka nahi dikhta. Image retina ke peeche banti hai. Correction: Convex Lens.
PAGE 6-10: OPTICS

4. Prism & Atmospheric Refraction

White light ka split hona (Dispersion) aur stars ka chamakna (Twinkling) sab isi category mein aate hain.

- Dispersion: Splitting of white light into 7 colors (VIBGYOR).
- Twinkling: Atmosphere ki layers ki density change hone se star ki light refract hoti rehti hai.
- Tyndall Effect: Scattering of light by dust/smoke particles.
PAGE 5: ADVANCED ANATOMY

Photoreceptors & Signal Transmission

Retina koi mamuli screen nahi hai. Isme cells ka pura network hota hai jo light ko bijli (electricity) mein badalta hai.

  • Rods: Ye cells "Light Intensity" (kam ya zyada roshni) ko pehchante hain. Raat ko rods hi kaam aate hain.
  • Cones: Ye "Colours" ko pehchante hain. Agar cones na ho, toh sab black and white dikhega.
  • Blind Spot: Jahan Optic Nerve eye se bahar nikalta hai, wahan koi image nahi banti.
Signal Flow: Light → Cornea → Pupil → Lens → Retina (Image) → Electrical Signal → Optic Nerve → Brain Interpretation.
PAGE 6: PRISM GEOMETRY

Refraction Through a Glass Prism

Glass slab aur Prism mein farak ye hai ki Prism ke sides parallel nahi hote, isliye light bend ho kar Base ki taraf jati hai.

$\angle A + \angle \delta = \angle i + \angle e$

Jahan $A$ = Angle of Prism aur $\delta$ = Angle of Deviation hai.

Angle of Deviation ($\delta$) depend karta hai:
1. Angle of Incidence ($\angle i$) par.
2. Prism ke material (Refractive Index) par.
3. Light ke colour (Wavelength) par.
PAGE 7: SPECTRUM

Newton's Double Prism Experiment

Sir Isaac Newton ne dikhaya ki white light 7 colors se bani hai. Unhone ek ulta prism use karke colors ko wapas white light mein badal diya.

VIBGYOR: Violet (sabse zyada bend), Red (sabse kam bend).

Rainbow Formation: Raindrop ek chote prism ki tarah kaam karti hai. Isme 3 cheezein hoti hain:
1. Refraction
2. Dispersion
3. Total Internal Reflection (TIR)
PAGE 8: ATMOSPHERE

Twinkling vs No Twinkling

Taare (Stars) timtimate hain, par Grah (Planets) nahi. Kyun?

Stars: Point source hote hain. Atmosphere ki layers hilne se light refract hoti hai aur lagta hai position change ho rahi hai.
Planets: Ye hamare bahut paas hain (Extended Source). Inse aane wali light ka total effect cancel ho jata hai, isliye ye twinkle nahi karte.
PAGE 9: SCATTERING

Tyndall Effect & Scattering

Jab light dhool (dust) ya dhuein (smoke) se takrati hai, toh wo scatter ho jati hai.

Rayleigh Scattering Law: Scattering $\propto 1/\lambda^4$. Matlab jiski wavelength $(\lambda)$ choti hogi, wo zyada scatter hoga.

Red Color: Iski wavelength sabse lamba (Longest) hoti hai. Isliye dhund (fog) ya dhuein mein ye scatter nahi hota aur bahut door tak dikhayi deta hai.
PAGE 10: SUNSET/SUNRISE

Apparent Flattening of Sun's Disc

Sunrise aur Sunset ke time Sun thoda chapta (flat) dikhta hai aur reddish dikhta hai.

1. Reddish Sun: Light ko lamba safar tay karna padta hai. Blue light scatter ho jati hai, sirf Red bachti hai.
2. Flattening: Horizon ke paas atmospheric refraction ki wajah se Sun ka upper aur lower edge alag alag bend hota hai.

SECTION: LONG ANSWER QUESTIONS (5 MARKS)

LONG Q1

Explain the structure and functioning of the Human Eye with a neat labeled diagram.

Describe the role of Cornea, Iris, Pupil, Lens, and Retina in the process of vision.

Answer Highlights:
1. Cornea: Outer transparent part, maximum refraction occurs here.
2. Iris & Pupil: Iris is a dark muscular diaphragm that controls the size of the pupil to regulate light.
3. Eye Lens: Adjusts focal length to form a sharp image on the retina.
4. Retina: Acts as a screen containing light-sensitive cells (Rods & Cones).
5. Optic Nerve: Transmits electrical signals to the brain.
LONG Q2

What is Myopia? State its causes. How can it be corrected? Explain with ray diagrams.

Also, explain why a person with myopia cannot see distant objects clearly.

Answer Highlights:
- Definition: A defect where a person sees nearby objects clearly but distant objects appear blurry.
- Causes: 1. Excessive curvature of the eye lens. 2. Elongation of the eyeball.
- Image formation: Image is formed in front of the retina.
- Correction: Using a Concave Lens of suitable focal length which diverges the rays to focus exactly on the retina.
LONG Q3

Explain the phenomenon of Dispersion of white light through a glass prism. Why do we see a spectrum?

Include Newton's experiment of recombination of spectrum to get white light back.

Answer Highlights:
- Dispersion: Splitting of white light into its seven constituent colors (VIBGYOR).
- Reason: Different colors of light travel at different speeds in glass, so they bend at different angles.
- Newton's Experiment: He used two prisms, one upright and one inverted. The inverted prism recombined the seven colors to form white light again, proving sunlight is made of 7 colors.
LONG Q4

How is a Rainbow formed in the sky? Explain the three physical processes involved.

Draw a diagram showing the path of a ray of light through a water droplet.

Answer Highlights:
A rainbow is a natural spectrum appearing in the sky after rain. Processes involved:
1. Refraction & Dispersion: Sunlight enters the raindrop and splits into colors.
2. Internal Reflection: Light hits the back of the drop and reflects back.
3. Refraction: Light exits the drop, bending again before reaching the observer's eye.
Note: Rainbow is always formed in the direction opposite to the Sun.
LONG Q5

Give reasons for the following atmospheric phenomena:
(a) Twinkling of Stars (b) Advanced Sunrise (c) Why Sky is Blue.

Answer Highlights:
(a) Twinkling: Due to atmospheric refraction. Refractive index of air layers keeps changing, causing the apparent position of the star to fluctuate.
(b) Advanced Sunrise: Atmosphere bends sunlight, making the sun visible 2 minutes before it actually crosses the horizon.
(c) Blue Sky: Scattering of light. Blue light has a shorter wavelength and is scattered more strongly by fine particles in the atmosphere than red light.
LONG Q6

What is Hypermetropia? Write its two causes. How is it corrected? Explain with the help of a ray diagram.

A person with a hypermetropic eye has a near point of 1 m. What is the power of the lens required to correct this defect? (Assume near point of normal eye is 25 cm).

Theory: Far-sightedness where a person can't see nearby objects. Causes: 1. Focal length of eye lens is too long. 2. Eyeball has become too small.
Correction: Convex lens.
Numerical: $u = -25 cm$, $v = -100 cm$.
Using $1/f = 1/v - 1/u$:
$1/f = 1/-100 - 1/-25 = -1/100 + 1/25 = 3/100$.
$f = 100/3 = 33.3 cm$.
Power (P) = +3.0 D.
LONG Q7

(a) Why does the Sun appear reddish early in the morning?
(b) Why does the Sun appear white at noon?

Explain the role of scattering of light in these observations.

(a) Sunrise: Light has to travel a larger distance through the atmosphere. Most of the blue/short wavelength light is scattered away. Only red light (longer wavelength) reaches our eyes.
(b) Noon: The Sun is overhead. Light travels a shorter distance. Very little scattering occurs, so all colors reach us almost equally, making it look white.
LONG Q8

Define 'Power of Accommodation'. Explain how ciliary muscles help in focusing objects at different distances.

What happens to the eye lens when we look at (i) a distant tree and (ii) a book held close to the eyes?

Definition: The ability of the eye lens to adjust its focal length to see both near and far objects.
(i) Distant Tree: Ciliary muscles relax → Lens becomes thin → Focal length increases → Sharp image on retina.
(ii) Close Book: Ciliary muscles contract → Lens becomes thick/curved → Focal length decreases → Sharp image on retina.
LONG Q9

Explain the formation of a spectrum by a glass prism. If a second identical prism is placed in an inverted position, what will happen?

Draw a diagram to illustrate the recombination of white light.

Dispersion: White light splits into VIBGYOR because different colors bend at different angles due to different speeds in glass.
Recombination: This was Newton's experiment. The first prism disperses light. The second (inverted) prism recombines these colors. The emergent ray from the second prism is white light again.
LONG Q10

Describe an activity to demonstrate the scattering of light in a colloidal solution (Tyndall Effect).

How does this explain the blue color of the sky and the reddish color of the sun at sunset?

Activity: Pass a beam of light through a tank of clear water, then add sodium thiosulphate and sulphuric acid. Sulphur particles start forming.
Observation: Blue light is scattered first (visible from sides). Red light is transmitted (visible from the other end).
Conclusion: This proves that fine particles scatter shorter wavelengths (blue) more easily, while longer wavelengths (red) pass through.

SECTION: HARD QUESTIONS (11-18)

HARD Q11

How does the eye focus on objects at different distances? Explain the role of the ciliary muscles and the crystalline lens in detail.

Discuss the change in curvature and focal length during this process.

Mechanism: To see distant objects, ciliary muscles relax, making the lens thin and increasing focal length. To see nearby objects, muscles contract, making the lens thick/curved, which decreases focal length to focus the image on the retina.
HARD Q12

Explain why a person with a normal eye cannot see clearly objects placed closer than 25 cm.

Relate this to the limit of accommodation of the eye lens.

Reason: Ciliary muscles cannot contract beyond a certain limit to make the lens thicker. If an object is closer than 25 cm, the lens cannot decrease its focal length enough to form a sharp image on the retina, resulting in a blurred image.
HARD Q13

Describe the formation of a spectrum using two identical glass prisms—one in an upright and the other in an inverted position.

Explain the observations made by Isaac Newton in this experiment.

Observation: The first prism disperses white light into seven colors. The second inverted prism recombines these colors back into a single beam of white light. This proved that white light is composed of seven constituent colors.
HARD Q14

Why do we see a rainbow only in the direction opposite to the sun? Explain the internal physics.

Include the concept of Total Internal Reflection (TIR).

Physics: Water droplets act as tiny prisms. Light enters, refracts/disperses, undergoes TIR at the back surface of the drop, and refracts again while exiting. This geometry only allows the observer to see the spectrum when the sun is behind them.
HARD Q15

Compare Myopia and Hypermetropia based on: (i) Nature of defect (ii) Causes (iii) Correction method.

Myopia: Near vision clear; caused by long eyeball/high curvature; corrected by Concave Lens.
Hypermetropia: Distant vision clear; caused by short eyeball/low curvature; corrected by Convex Lens.
HARD Q16

What is Presbyopia? How is it different from Hypermetropia? Suggest a remedy for a person with both defects.

Presbyopia: Age-related weakening of ciliary muscles. Unlike Hypermetropia (eyeball size issue), this is a loss of flexibility. People with both Myopia and Presbyopia use Bifocal Lenses (Upper part concave, lower part convex).
HARD Q17

Explain the 'Twinkling of Stars' and why planets do not twinkle using the concept of apparent position.

Explanation: Stars are point sources; atmospheric turbulence shifts their apparent position, causing twinkling. Planets are closer/extended sources; the average brightness from many points remains constant, canceling the twinkling effect.
HARD Q18

Numerical: A person cannot see objects beyond 2 m clearly. Identify the defect and calculate the power of lens required.

Defect: Myopia.
Calculation: $v = -2 m$, $u = \infty$.
$1/f = 1/v - 1/u = 1/-2 - 1/\infty = -1/2$.
$f = -2 m$.
Power (P) = 1/f = -0.5 D (Concave lens).
