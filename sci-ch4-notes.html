CARBON & ITS COMPOUNDS

PAGE 1: BONDING

The Versatile Nature of Carbon

Carbon form Covalent Bonds. Why not Ionic?

  • It cannot lose 4 electrons (needs too much energy).
  • It cannot gain 4 electrons (nucleus cannot hold 10 electrons).
  • So, it SHARES electrons.
PAGE 2: UNIQUE PROPERTIES

Catenation & Tetravalency

  • Catenation: Unique ability to form long chains with other Carbon atoms.
  • Tetravalency: Having 4 valence electrons, it can bond with four other atoms.
PAGE 3: HYDROCARBONS

Saturated & Unsaturated

Alkanes: Single bonds (C-C). Formula: CₙH₂ₙ₊₂

Alkenes: Double bonds (C=C). Formula: CₙH₂ₙ

Alkynes: Triple bonds (C≡C). Formula: CₙH₂ₙ₋₂

Methane: CH4 | Ethane: C2H6 | Ethene: C2H4
PAGE 4: SERIES

Homologous Series

A group of compounds with the same functional group and similar chemical properties.

  • Difference between two adjacent members is -CH₂.
  • Difference in molecular mass is 14u.
PAGE 5: NOMENCLATURE

Functional Groups

  • Alcohol: -OH (Suffix: -ol)
  • Aldehyde: -CHO (Suffix: -al)
  • Ketone: >C=O (Suffix: -one)
  • Carboxylic Acid: -COOH (Suffix: -oic acid)
PAGE 6: STRUCTURES

Structural Isomers

Compounds with the same molecular formula but different structures.

Example: Butane (C4H10) has 2 isomers: n-butane and iso-butane.
PAGE 7: REACTIONS

Combustion & Oxidation

Carbon compounds burn in oxygen to give CO₂, water, and energy.

CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O + Heat + Light

Oxidation: Alcohols are converted to carboxylic acids using Alkaline KMnO₄.

PAGE 8: ADDITION & SUBSTITUTION

Addition & Substitution

  • Addition Reaction: Unsaturated hydrocarbons add Hydrogen to become saturated (Hydrogenation of Oils).
  • Substitution Reaction: Saturated hydrocarbons react with Chlorine in sunlight.
PAGE 9: IMPORTANT COMPOUNDS

Ethanol (C₂H₅OH)

Liquid at room temp, good solvent.

  • Reaction with Sodium: Evolves H₂ gas.
  • Dehydration: Heating with Conc. H₂SO₄ gives Ethene.
PAGE 10: ACETIC ACID

Ethanoic Acid (CH₃COOH)

Commonly known as Acetic Acid. 5-8% solution is called Vinegar.

  • Esterification: Acid + Alcohol → Ester (Sweet smelling).
  • Saponification: Ester + NaOH → Soap + Alcohol.
PAGE 11: CLEANSING AGENTS

Soaps & Micelles

Soaps are sodium/potassium salts of long chain fatty acids.

  • Hydrophilic head: Loves water.
  • Hydrophobic tail: Loves oil/dirt.
Micelles form a cluster to trap dirt in the center.
PAGE 12: HARD WATER

Detergents

Used for washing in Hard Water. They do not form "scum" with Calcium and Magnesium salts present in hard water.

SECTION C: CH-4 SUBJECTIVE QUESTIONS

Q1. Explain the mechanism of the cleansing action of soaps. (Micelle Formation)
Most dirt is oily in nature. Soap molecules have two ends with different properties:

1. Hydrophilic end: The ionic end that dissolves in water.
2. Hydrophobic end: The hydrocarbon chain that dissolves in oil/dirt.

When soap is at the surface of water, the hydrophobic 'tails' align towards the center of a cluster, trapping the dirt inside, while the ionic 'heads' face outwards. This structure is called a Micelle.

The micelle stays in the water as a colloid and the dirt attached to the tails is easily washed away when the cloth is agitated.
Q2. What is a Homologous Series? List its any three characteristics.
Definition: A series of carbon compounds in which the same functional group substitutes for hydrogen in a carbon chain is called a Homologous Series.

Characteristics:
1. Any two adjacent members differ by a -CH2 unit.
2. The difference in molecular mass between two successive members is 14u.
3. All members of the series show similar chemical properties because they have the same functional group.
4. There is a gradual change in physical properties (like melting/boiling points) as molecular mass increases.
Q3. Describe Esterification and Saponification reactions with chemical equations.
Esterification: When Ethanoic acid reacts with absolute Ethanol in the presence of an acid catalyst, a sweet-smelling substance called an 'Ester' is formed.
Equation: CH3COOH + C2H5OH → CH3COOC2H5 + H2O

Saponification: When an Ester reacts with Sodium Hydroxide (NaOH), it is converted back into alcohol and the sodium salt of carboxylic acid (Soap).
Equation: CH3COOC2H5 + NaOH → CH3COONa + C2H5OH

This process is used in the preparation of soaps.
Q4. Why does Carbon form compounds mainly by covalent bonding? Explain Catenation.
Carbon has 4 electrons in its outermost shell. To achieve stability:
1. It cannot gain 4 electrons to form C4- because it's difficult for 6 protons to hold 10 electrons.
2. It cannot lose 4 electrons to form C4+ because it requires a huge amount of energy.
Therefore, carbon overcomes this by sharing electrons, resulting in covalent bonds.

Catenation: Carbon has the unique ability to form bonds with other atoms of carbon, giving rise to large molecules. This property is called catenation. It can form long chains, branched chains, or even rings.
Q5. Write the chemical properties of Ethanol with balanced equations.
1. Reaction with Sodium: Ethanol reacts with sodium to form sodium ethoxide and hydrogen gas.
Equation: 2Na + 2CH3CH2OH → 2CH3CH2ONa + H2

2. Dehydration: Heating ethanol at 443 K with excess concentrated sulphuric acid results in the formation of ethene.
Equation: CH3CH2OH (Conc. H2SO4 / Heat) → CH2=CH2 + H2O

3. Oxidation: Ethanol is oxidized to ethanoic acid in the presence of alkaline KMnO4.
Equation: CH3CH2OH (Alk. KMnO4 + Heat) → CH3COOH
Q6. What are Structural Isomers? Draw the structures of isomers of Butane.
Definition: Compounds having the same molecular formula but different structural arrangements of atoms are called structural isomers.

Isomers of Butane (C4H10):
1. n-Butane: A straight chain of 4 carbon atoms.
(CH3—CH2—CH2—CH3)

2. Iso-butane (2-Methylpropane): A branched chain where 3 carbons are in a row and 1 is attached to the middle carbon.
(CH3—CH(CH3)—CH3)

As the number of carbon atoms increases, the number of possible isomers also increases.
Q7. Distinguish between Soaps and Detergents. Why do soaps not work in hard water?
Soaps: Sodium/potassium salts of long-chain fatty acids. They are biodegradable but not effective in hard water.

Detergents: Sodium salts of long-chain benzene sulphonic acids. They work in both soft and hard water.

Hard Water Problem: Hard water contains Calcium (Ca2+) and Magnesium (Mg2+) ions. Soaps react with these ions to form an insoluble, sticky precipitate called SCUM. This scum sticks to the cloth and makes the cleaning process difficult. Detergents do not form scum.
Q8. What is the difference between Saturated and Unsaturated Hydrocarbons?
1. Bonding: Saturated hydrocarbons have only single bonds (C—C), while unsaturated ones have at least one double (C=C) or triple (C≡C) bond.

2. Reactivity: Saturated hydrocarbons are generally unreactive (Paraffins), whereas unsaturated ones are very reactive.

3. Combustion: Saturated hydrocarbons usually burn with a clean blue flame. Unsaturated ones burn with a yellow sooty flame due to incomplete combustion.

4. Examples: Alkanes (Methane) are saturated. Alkenes (Ethene) and Alkynes (Ethyne) are unsaturated.

🔥 ELITE CHALLENGE: 5 HARDEST QUESTIONS

Q1. An organic compound 'A' (C2H4O2) reacts with Sodium metal to form 'B' and evolves H2 gas. 'A' on heating with Ethanol and acid forms 'C' (sweet smell). 'C' on treating with NaOH gives back 'B' and Ethanol. Identify A, B, C and write all equations.
Identification:
• A is Ethanoic Acid (CH3COOH).
• B is Sodium Ethanoate (CH3COONa).
• C is Ethyl Ethanoate (CH3COOC2H5 - an Ester).

Equations:
1. 2CH3COOH + 2Na → 2CH3COONa + H2 (Reaction with Na)
2. CH3COOH + C2H5OH → CH3COOC2H5 + H2O (Esterification)
3. CH3COOC2H5 + NaOH → CH3COONa + C2H5OH (Saponification)
Q2. Why is the conversion of Ethanol to Ethanoic Acid called an Oxidation reaction? Explain the role of Alkaline KMnO4 in detail.
Oxidation is the addition of oxygen or removal of hydrogen. In this process:
CH3CH2OH + 2[O] → CH3COOH + H2O

The ethanol molecule gains one oxygen atom and loses two hydrogen atoms, hence it is oxidation.

Role of Alkaline KMnO4: It acts as a Strong Oxidising Agent. It provides the nascent oxygen required for the reaction. It first oxidizes ethanol to ethanal, and then immediately to ethanoic acid. The alkaline medium ensures the reaction proceeds efficiently.
Q3. Draw all possible structural isomers for Bromopentane (C5H11Br). Why can't we have isomers for Methane or Ethane?
Isomers of C5H11Br: There are 8 isomers total (Chain + Position). Major ones are:
1. 1-Bromopentane (Straight chain)
2. 2-Bromopentane (Br on 2nd Carbon)
3. 3-Bromopentane (Br on 3rd Carbon)
4. 1-Bromo-2-methylbutane (Branched), and so on.

Logic: Methane (1C) and Ethane (2C) do not show isomerism because there is only one way to arrange 1 or 2 carbon atoms in a chain. Branching requires a minimum of 3 carbons (for functional groups) or 4 carbons (for chain branching).
Q4. Explain the chemical reason behind 'Scum' formation. How does the chemical structure of Detergents solve this problem?
Scum Chemistry: Hard water contains Ca2+ and Mg2+ ions. Soap is Sodium Stearate. When they react, they form Calcium/Magnesium Stearate, which is insoluble in water and precipitates as a sticky grey mass called scum.

Detergent Solution: Detergents are generally ammonium or sulphonate salts of long-chain carboxylic acids. The charged ends of these compounds do not form insoluble precipitates with the calcium and magnesium ions in hard water. Thus, they remain effective even in hard water.
Q5. You are given two liquids: Ethanol and Ethanoic Acid. How would you distinguish between them using (a) Litmus test, and (b) Sodium bicarbonate test? Write the logic.
(a) Litmus Test:
• Ethanoic Acid will turn blue litmus red (it's an acid).
• Ethanol will show no change (it's neutral to litmus).

(b) Sodium Bicarbonate (NaHCO3) Test:
• Ethanoic Acid reacts with NaHCO3 to produce Brisk Effervescence due to the evolution of CO2 gas.
• Ethanol does not react with NaHCO3, so no bubbles are seen.

CH3COOH + NaHCO3 → CH3COONa + H2O + CO2↑

⚡ GOD-MODE ACTIVATED: FINAL 5 IMPOSSIBLE QUESTIONS ⚡

Q1. Explain why Carbon cannot form a quadruple bond (C≡≡C) with another carbon atom. Discuss the role of bond angles in Covalent molecules.
Carbon has 4 valence electrons. While it forms single, double, and triple bonds, a Quadruple bond is impossible because:
1. Steric Hindrance: Bringing 8 electrons (4 pairs) between two small carbon nuclei creates massive electronic repulsion.
2. Orbital Geometry: Carbon uses p-orbitals to form pi-bonds. After forming one sigma and two pi bonds (triple bond), there are no more orbitals oriented in a way to form a fourth stable bond.

Bond Angles: In Methane, the angle is 109.5° (Tetrahedral). In Ethene, it is 120° (Planar). In Ethyne, it is 180° (Linear). These angles minimize repulsion between electron pairs.
Q2. What happens when Ethanol is treated with acidified Potassium Dichromate? How is this reaction different from the reaction with Conc. H2SO4 at 443K?
These are two completely different chemical pathways:

1. With Acidified K2Cr2O7 (Oxidation): Ethanol is converted into Ethanoic Acid. K2Cr2O7 acts as an oxidising agent, changing color from Orange to Green.
CH3CH2OH + 2[O] → CH3COOH + H2O

2. With Conc. H2SO4 at 443K (Dehydration): Here, H2SO4 acts as a Dehydrating Agent, removing a water molecule to form an unsaturated hydrocarbon (Ethene).
CH3CH2OH → CH2=CH2 + H2O
Q3. Graphite is soft and a conductor, while Diamond is hard and an insulator. Both are made of Carbon. Explain this paradox using their internal bonding architecture.
The difference lies in Structure, not Composition:

• Diamond: Each carbon is bonded to 4 others in a 3D Tetrahedral structure. All valence electrons are locked in strong covalent bonds. Result: Extreme hardness and no free electrons for conductivity.

• Graphite: Each carbon is bonded to only 3 others in hexagonal layers. The 4th electron of each carbon is FREE/DELOCALIZED. These layers are held by weak Van der Waals forces, allowing them to slide. Result: Softness/Lubricant property and high electrical conductivity.
Q4. A person uses soap in water with high Magnesium content. He observes a white sticky layer on his skin after washing. Identify the chemical 'X' formed and explain the chemical equation behind it.
The white sticky layer is SCUM. Specifically, it is Magnesium Stearate.

Chemical Equation:
2C17H35COONa (Soap) + Mg2+ (from Hard Water) → (C17H35COO)2Mg (Scum) + 2Na+

Logic: Soap is a sodium salt. In hard water, the Sodium ions are displaced by Magnesium or Calcium ions. The resulting Magnesium salt is Insoluble in water, making the soap useless for cleaning until all Mg2+ ions are precipitated.
Q5. Draw and name the structural isomers of Propanol (C3H7OH). Can Propanol show 'Chain Isomerism'? Justify your answer.
Propanol has two Position Isomers:
1. Propan-1-ol: CH3—CH2—CH2—OH (OH on the end carbon).
2. Propan-2-ol: CH3—CH(OH)—CH3 (OH on the middle carbon).

Justification: Propanol CANNOT show Chain Isomerism. To show chain isomerism (branching of the carbon backbone), you need at least 4 Carbon atoms (as in Butane). With only 3 carbons, any "branch" you try to make will still result in a continuous 3-carbon chain.
