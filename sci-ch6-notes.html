PAGE 1-2: THE NEURON

Nervous System &
Nerve Cells

All information from our environment is detected by specialized tips of some nerve cells called Receptors.

Impulse Path:
Dendrite → Cell Body → Axon → Nerve Ending → Synapse.

At the Synapse, a chemical signal is released to bridge the gap between two neurons.

PAGE 3-4: REFLEXES

Reflex Action &
Reflex Arc

Sudden responses to stimuli that bypass the conscious brain for speed are Reflex Actions.

  • Sensory Neuron: Carries signal to spinal cord.
  • Motor Neuron: Carries command to muscle.
  • Effector: The muscle that performs the action.
PAGE 5-6: HUMAN BRAIN

The Central Nervous System

Brain is the main coordinating center. It is protected by the Cranium (skull) and CSF.

1. Fore-brain: Thinking, memory, and voluntary actions.
2. Mid-brain: Involuntary reflexes (eye/head).
3. Hind-brain: Cerebellum (Balance), Medulla (BP/Vomiting), and Pons (Respiration).
PAGE 7-8: PLANTS

Coordination in Plants

Plants use hormones for communication. They show Tropic Movements (Growth-related).

  • Phototropism: Movement towards light (Auxin).
  • Geotropism: Response to gravity.
  • Chemotropism: Growth of pollen tubes.
PAGE 9-10: HORMONES

Hormones in Animals

Endocrine glands release hormones directly into the blood.

- Adrenaline: Emergency hormone.
- Thyroxine: Regulates metabolism (Needs Iodine).
- Insulin: Controls blood sugar levels.
- Growth Hormone: Secreted by Pituitary gland.

The amount is regulated by the Feedback Mechanism.

PRACTICE MCQS

LONG QUESTIONS

LONG Q1
Draw the structure of a neuron and explain its functions. How does the impulse travel?

Answer: Neuron has three main parts: Dendrite (acquires info), Cell Body (processes info), and Axon (transmits info).

Impulse Travel: Info creates an electrical impulse at dendrite → travels to axon → at synapse, chemicals are released → start new impulse in next neuron.

LONG Q2
What is a reflex arc? Trace the sequence of events when we touch a hot object.

Answer: The pathway of nerve impulses in a reflex action is a reflex arc.

Sequence: Stimulus (Heat) → Receptor (Skin) → Sensory Neuron → Spinal Cord → Motor Neuron → Effector (Muscle) → Response (Hand pulls back).

LONG Q3
Explain the major regions of the human brain and their specific functions.

Answer:
1. Fore-brain: Main thinking part, controls voluntary actions and memory.
2. Mid-brain: Controls involuntary head and eye movements.
3. Hind-brain: Cerebellum (balance), Medulla (BP, vomiting), and Pons (respiration).

LONG Q4
Differentiate between Tropic and Nastic movements in plants with examples.

Answer:
- Tropic: Directional and growth-dependent. Ex: Phototropism (stem moves to light).
- Nastic: Non-directional and growth-independent. Ex: Drooping of Touch-me-not leaves.

LONG Q5
How does our body respond when adrenaline is secreted into the blood?

Answer: It prepares the body for emergency:
1. Heart beat increases to supply more oxygen.
2. Blood diverted to skeletal muscles.
3. Breathing rate increases. This is the "Fight or Flight" response.

LONG Q6
How do auxins promote the growth of a tendril around a support?

Answer: Auxin diffuses towards the side away from the support. This causes the outer side to grow faster, making the tendril circle the support.

LONG Q7
Why is the use of iodised salt advisable?

Answer: Iodine is needed by the Thyroid gland to make Thyroxine. It prevents Goitre.

LONG Q8
Explain the feedback mechanism for regulation of insulin secretion.

Answer: When blood sugar levels rise, it is detected by the cells of the Pancreas which respond by producing more insulin. As the blood sugar level falls, insulin secretion is reduced. This timing and amount of hormone released are controlled by the feedback mechanism.

LONG Q9
Compare and contrast Nervous and Hormonal coordination.

Answer:
1. Nervous: Information is sent as electrical impulses. It is very fast but its effect is short-lived.
2. Hormonal: Information is sent as chemical messengers via blood. It is slower but reaches every cell and has a long-lasting effect.

LONG Q10
What are the functions of Cerebellum and Medulla?

Answer:
- Cerebellum: Controls precision of voluntary actions and maintains the posture and balance of the body.
- Medulla: Controls involuntary actions such as blood pressure, salivation, and vomiting.

HARD Q11
How does the movement of leaves in a sensitive plant (Mimosa pudica) differ from the movement of a shoot towards light?

Answer:
1. Sensitive Plant: It is a Nastic movement. It is growth-independent and caused by sudden change in turgor pressure (water amount) in leaves. It is very fast.
2. Shoot towards light: It is a Tropic movement (Phototropism). It is growth-dependent and caused by the unequal distribution of Auxin hormone. It is a very slow process.

HARD Q12
Explain the mechanism of 'Chemical Communication' in animals. Why is it needed alongside nervous coordination?

Answer: Chemical communication happens through Hormones secreted by endocrine glands directly into blood.

Why needed? Nervous impulses do not reach every single cell of the body. Also, nerve cells cannot constantly create and transmit impulses. Hormones can reach each and every cell and provide long-lasting, steady coordination.

HARD Q13
What happens at the synapse between two neurons? Explain the role of neurotransmitters.

Answer: Synapse is the microscopic gap between two neurons. When an electrical impulse reaches the nerve ending of an axon, it triggers the release of Neurotransmitters (chemicals). These chemicals diffuse across the gap and start a similar electrical impulse in the dendrite of the next neuron.

HARD Q14
How does the brain and spinal cord stay protected? Mention the layers and fluid.

Answer:
1. Brain: It is situtated inside a bony box called the Cranium (Skull). Inside it, the brain is contained in a fluid-filled balloon which acts as a shock absorber. This fluid is Cerebrospinal Fluid (CSF).
2. Spinal Cord: It is protected by a bony structure called the Vertebral Column or backbone.

HARD Q15
Explain how the human eye adjusts to look at distant and nearby objects. Which part of the brain coordinates this?

Answer: The ciliary muscles modify the curvature of the eye lens to adjust its focal length (Power of Accommodation).

Brain Part: The Mid-brain is responsible for coordinating these visual reflexes and involuntary eye muscle movements.

HARD Q16
What is the significance of the 'Blood-Brain Barrier' and how is the CNS protected from mechanical shocks?

Answer: The BBB protects the brain from harmful chemicals and pathogens in the blood.

Mechanical Protection: The brain is floating in Cerebrospinal Fluid (CSF) inside the cranium. This fluid acts as a shock absorber, spreading the force of an impact to prevent direct injury to the delicate brain tissue.

HARD Q17
Why is the pancreas called a 'Dual Gland'? Explain its role in maintaining homeostasis.

Answer: It is both Exocrine (secretes digestive enzymes via ducts) and Endocrine (secretes hormones like Insulin and Glucagon directly into blood).

Homeostasis: It maintains blood sugar levels. Insulin lowers high sugar, while Glucagon increases low sugar, ensuring a stable internal environment.

HARD Q18
Describe the path of a nerve impulse in the body. Why can it only flow in one direction?

Answer: Path: Dendrite → Cell Body → Axon → Synapse → Next Neuron.

One-way Flow: This is because neurotransmitters are only present in the Axon Terminals (nerve endings) and receptors for these chemicals are only present on the Dendrites of the next neuron. Thus, the signal cannot go backward.
